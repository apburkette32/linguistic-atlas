The Linguistic Atlas of New England

Authored by: apburkette32 for New Maps Plus

The Linguistic Atlas of New England is a regional subsurvey of the Linguistic Atlas Project (LAP), a linguistic survey of American English that began in 1929 and continues today. LANE was the first of the Atlas surveys, with interviews conducted between 1931 and 1933. The New England speakers that were interviewed were asked a seriesof questions about words for things: items found around the house and farm, different kinds of weather, social relationships, etc. The map featured here represents the LANE speakers' answers to the question, "What do you call the piece of furniture in your bedroom that has drawers and is where you keep folded items, socks, and such?"

Enlarge map

    LANE and the other regional LAP surveys are now housed at the University of Kentucky. Some of the LAP holdings are digital, but much is paper, including original lithograph maps that were hand-drawn in the 1930s as a way to display the linguistic data collected.
